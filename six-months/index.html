Month one · Dubai

We started at the top of the world.

Glass, gold, and a city that looks like the future turned up early. Week one of being married and we were already somewhere neither of us had been.

Month one · Egypt

Then somewhere much older.

Egypt, standing in front of things that were already ancient when the rest of the world was new. Sand in everything. Worth it.

Two countries in, and I already knew this was going to be a good story.

Months two & three

Then came the quiet part.

You went to work. I sent applications. The winter went on longer than it had any right to.

The waiting

You never made me feel behind.

Two months of refreshing an inbox that mostly stayed empty. You kept showing up, kept saying it would happen, and somehow turned the waiting into something we were doing together instead of something I was doing alone.

Inbox — no new messages

Month four · The offer

And then everything turned at once.

The job came. I read the email about four times before I told you, and you were somehow less surprised than I was.

Month four · The keys

Then a door that was only ours.

Our own place. No roommates, no in-between. First night was takeout on the floor with nothing unpacked, and it still felt like the best address in Ontario.

Just the two of us, finally.

19 May · Your birthday

And the forest showed up for it.

We drove into Algonquin on your birthday and the park behaved like it knew.

Algonquin Provincial Park

A moose. An actual moose.

Standing there like it had been waiting for us. Then bears. Then deer at the edge of the road, completely unbothered. I'm still not over the fact that this is the birthday you got.

Moose Bears Deer

Happy birthday, again. The whole park turned out for you.

Month five · Banff

Mountains next, obviously.

Water in a colour that shouldn't be real. Peaks that make you go quiet for a second before anyone says anything.

Month five · Jasper

And the long drive up to Jasper.

Hours of road, the playlist running out, and us just talking. Everything around us was enormous and you were still the best thing in the frame.

Small in front of mountains, in the best possible way.
